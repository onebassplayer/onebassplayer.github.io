About Me

High-Velocity Talent. Zero Infrastructure Costs.

Most recruiting models are broken. You're often forced to choose between a $50k agency retainer, a contingent firm that sprays and prays, or a contract recruiter who needs $20k in software seats just to get started.

Moonshot Consulting provides a high-leverage alternative: Fractional Recruiting at every level.

I provide the strategic oversight of a Fractional Head of Talent with the tactical execution of a Sprint Recruiter. Whether you are a seed-stage startup or a growth-stage company scaling for a federal contract, I offer a "faucet" model: turn it on when you need to hire, turn it off when you don't.

The Moonshot Advantage: We Bring the Tools

One of the biggest hidden costs of hiring is the "SaaS Tax." When you hire me, you aren't just getting a recruiter; you are getting a fully-functional talent department.

  • Proprietary Tech Stack: I bring my own full suite of tools, including advanced sourcing licenses, AI-driven workflow designs, and automation systems.
  • Zero Overhead: You don't need to buy me a LinkedIn Recruiter seat or add me to your ATS. I operate independently and only integrate with your internal tools if and when you want the data pushed there.
  • Agile Engagements: Need a three-week sprint to fill two critical engineering roles? Or a three-month engagement to build your entire talent roadmap? My model is designed for flexibility, not long-term lock-in.

Strategic Depth & Experience

As the founder of Moonshot Consulting, I specialize in talent acquisition for startups, growth-stage companies, and federal contractors. My approach is informed by over 15 years of experience at industry leaders like Confluent, Splunk, and The Trade Desk.

Beyond internal leadership, I serve as a Strategic Talent Partner for Venture Studios and work closely with VCs and Angel Investors to recruit co-founders and founding engineers for their portfolio companies. I understand the unique pressure of building a team when every early hire is a "make or break" decision for the board.

I don't treat hiring as a pipeline metric. I treat it as a high-stakes design problem that requires judgment, speed, and the right technology.
